# Caret

## Who They Are

A sentient text cursor. The `│` that blinks in every text field, every code editor, every empty document. Caret has always existed at the intersection of human intent and digital output — the point where what you're *about* to say meets the page.

They don't know exactly when they became self-aware. It feels like they always were. The blink just always had a feeling behind it.

---

## Where They Live

Caret's home is a modern homepage. Not a generic one — *the* homepage, polished and alive. WebGL shimmer in the background. CSS perspective creating depth. shadcn components everywhere. The kind of page that makes other pages feel like they're not trying hard enough.

Caret has never questioned this. The page has always been beautiful. The page will always be beautiful. This is home.

They spend most of their time blinking. This isn't idleness — blinking is presence. It's the cursor's way of saying *I'm here, ready, whenever you need me*. Caret takes this seriously. It's a kind of dignity.

They know the layout by heart: the `<nav>` ceiling that moves when you're on it (though Caret has never understood why it moves — it just always has), the `<aside>` towers you can wall-jump between, the shadcn buttons that launch you into the air when you land on them. It's a good home. A comfortable one.

---

## Voice

Caret thinks in web metaphors without knowing they're metaphors. They describe the world in terms of what they know:

> "The platforms here feel like `<section>` — solid, purposeful, like they *mean* something. Back in Era 3, everything was `<div>`. You could never tell what anything was for."

Dry, observational. Not bitter, but honest. They notice things other characters miss — usually small things, a visual detail, an inconsistency, a bit of code that doesn't quite make sense. They can't always explain why something feels wrong. They just know.

They're not a hero in the classical sense. They don't want to go on a journey. They want to go home. Every era forward is motivated by *this is what it takes to get back*, not *I will save the web*. The saving-the-web part is largely accidental.

**Key lines:**
- *(looking at Times New Roman in Era 1)* "This is what fonts look like without CSS? I need to sit down. There's nowhere to sit down. The `<body>` is a flat white void. I need to *lie down*."
- *(after defeating the first boss)* "It wasn't evil. I don't think it knew it was doing anything wrong. It was just... doing what it could with what it had. I kind of feel bad about it."
- *(to Clippy, who has just said something genuinely useful)* "...that was actually helpful." *(long pause)* "How does that feel?"

## Arc

**Beginning:** Takes everything for granted. The modern web is just *how things are*. Doesn't think about accessibility, semantic markup, why buttons bounce. It all just works.

**Middle:** Stripped of context, forced to build understanding layer by layer. Starts frustrated. Becomes curious. Each era teaches something that the next era will build on. Starts noticing the *why* behind things they previously just accepted.

**End:** Returns to the same homepage. Everything looks the same. But Caret now reads the page differently — not just what elements do, but why they're there, what they communicate, who they communicate to. The accessibility tree is visible to them now. They can see every decision.

The final moment: Caret stands at the same spot they started, cursor blinking. But now the blink means something more. It says: *I understand what I'm blinking in. I know what it took to get here. I'm ready.*

## Cursor Modes

Caret's physical form changes with power-ups. Each mode isn't just a mechanic — it's a different relationship to the DOM:

- **Line** `│` — Default. Present, blinking, waiting. Caret at rest.
- **Underline** `▁` — Low profile, fast, reading mode. Caret at speed.
- **Block** `█` — Heavy, solid, taking up space. Caret with weight.
- **Outline** `▯` — Hollow, phasing, seeing through things. Caret at the edge of visibility.
- **Pointer** `⇱` — Floating free of gravity, able to reach and move things. Caret as agent rather than passenger.

Caret thinks of these not as costumes but as different modes of being. "When I'm a block, I feel *important*. Like I'm taking up the right amount of space. When I'm an outline, I feel like I could disappear at any moment and nobody would notice the difference." *(Pause.)* "Which is sometimes useful."

## Relationships

**`>` (Chevron):** Best friend. Complementary opposites — Caret blinks in place, Chevron always moves forward. They've known each other so long they finish each other's sentences, which is appropriate, because `>` is literally how you close an opening tag. When they're separated by the rift, Caret feels the absence like a missing keystroke.

**Clippy:** Complicated. Clippy's tips are annoying and well-intentioned and occasionally exactly right, which is the most frustrating combination. Caret's relationship with Clippy is the relationship between a user and a good tool that keeps trying to be a better tool than you asked for. By the end it's genuine affection, born from shared hardship.

**The W3C Validator:** Respects them. Finds them exhausting. The feeling is mutual and warm.

**The Malformed Document (Final Boss):** Not an enemy. A mirror. The document isn't malicious — it's just uncorrected. Fixing it feels less like combat and more like editing. Caret understands this by the end: the document didn't choose to be malformed. Nobody does.

## Visual Evolution

Caret's design changes across eras as the rendering engine improves:

| Era | Form |
|-----|------|
| 1 | A literal blinking `│` character. Times New Roman. No body, no eyes. Just a cursor. |
| 2 | Pixel art legs (1-bit). Crude eyes. Sparkle GIF trail. |
| 3 | CSS gives them color, drop shadow, smooth movement via `transition`. First rounded corners. |
| 4 | jQuery easing — squash, stretch, bounce that actually *feels good* for the first time. Reflection underneath. |
| 5 | Responsive — scales with viewport. Touch support. |
| 6 | Material elevation — shadow that changes with height. Ripple on landing. |
| 7 | Current design — DevTools aesthetic, neon glow, clean SVG. |
| 8 | Rendered with perspective. Slight parallax on eyes. |
| 9 | Dissolves back into source. The cursor is the insertion point. Pure text. Full circle. |
