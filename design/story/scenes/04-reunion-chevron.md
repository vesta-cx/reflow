# Scene 04 — Reunion: Chevron

*Finding `>` in Era 2 (Geocities). Chevron is stuck in a web ring — a circular chain of pages where each `<a>` link points to the next site in the ring, and the last points back to the first. Chevron has been navigating it for a long time.*

*This is the first major reunion and should feel like a real moment — not just a power-up unlock.*

---

The web ring occupies a small cul-de-sac of the Geocities level: three `<a>` links in a row, each one labeled **[Next Site]**, **[Previous Site]**, **[Random Site]**. Between them, tiny GIF images: spinning globes, animated "under construction" banners, a small dancing skeleton.

Caret has been through this section twice and almost missed it — it looks like background decoration. But on the third pass, something moves in the web ring that isn't an animation.

`>` is going around the loop. Not desperately — methodically. They've long since stopped expecting to find an exit and are now moving out of habit, or stubbornness, or an inability to remain still. They pass Caret without seeing them, follow the **[Next Site]** link, disappear for three seconds, reappear via **[Previous Site]**, and head for **[Next Site]** again.

> **Caret:** Chevron.

`>` stops. Turns. Stares.

*(A long moment where neither of them says anything.)*

*(Then:)*

> **`>`:** You're alive.
> **Caret:** I'm— yes. You thought I wasn't?
> **`>`:** You disappeared into an unknown element and the CSS reverted to defaults and then I fell off a platform because the `<aside>` reflowed and I couldn't wall-jump and then `#` stopped responding and then— *(they stop themselves)* — how long has it been for you?
> **Caret:** Two eras. Era 1 and most of Era 2.
> **`>`:** Four eras here. I've been in this web ring for *four eras*.
> **Caret:** ...the web ring didn't let you out?
> **`>`:** THE WEB RING DIDN'T LET ME OUT. I've been past that *(gestures at a tiled background GIF of flames)* seventeen thousand times. The seventeenth one is named Steve. They're all named Steve now. Time stopped having meaning.

*(Caret approaches the web ring. Inspects the links.)*

> **DOM Inspector:** `<a href="webring.html?next&id=3847">` — A hyperlink. Navigates to another page. This particular link is part of a web ring — a circular network of sites linked together by topic, popular in the 1990s. The web ring was an early discovery mechanism: how you found new sites before search engines. Following the ring was how you explored. **The ring has no exit because nobody thought to build one.**

> **Caret:** There's no exit. The ring was just... never designed to let you leave.
> **`>`:** I KNOW THERE'S NO EXIT. I've been in it for FOUR ERAS.
> **Caret:** I'm going to slash through one of these links.
> **`>`:** You can slash now?
> **Caret:** I found `/` in Era 1. Long story.
> **`>`:** Is `/` okay?
> **Caret:** Yes. Emotionally complicated, but okay.

*(Caret uses Slash's mechanic — closes one of the `<a>` elements in the ring. The ring breaks. A gap opens. `>` stares at the gap for a moment.)*

*(Then walks through it very quickly, as if afraid it'll close again.)*

*(On the other side, they stop. Look back at the web ring. The gap is there. The broken link. It's open.)*

> **`>`:** I could have done that myself.
> **Caret:** You don't have `/`. It's not your ability.
> **`>`:** I know. I just—

*(Long pause.)*

> **`>`:** I had a lot of time to think about why I couldn't leave. And the answer is "I didn't have the right tool," which is fine. That's a good answer. But the other answer is that I couldn't make myself turn around and look for another way out. I kept going forward into the same loop. Because that's what I do. I go forward.
> **Caret:** That's not a character flaw.
> **`>`:** It can be.
> **Caret:** It can also be the thing that gets us home.

*(They look at each other.)*

> **`>`:** How many eras?
> **Caret:** Seven left. Maybe more.
> **`>`:** *(exhaling)* Okay. Okay. Tell me what I missed.
> **Caret:** `<font>` was the Era 1 boss. It wasn't that bad. It kept making things white on white.
> **`>`:** Oh no.
> **Caret:** The `<font>` was genuinely sad about it.
> **`>`:** Of course it was.
> **Caret:** And the Webmaster is here. In Era 2 still. They speak entirely in raw HTML.
> **`>`:** How's that working out for them?
> **Caret:** They seem fine. They have a guestbook.
> **`>`:** Of course they do.

*(They walk forward together. Not quite the same as before — `>` has been alone for four eras, Caret has fought through two, they've both changed slightly. But the rhythm is still there. The push-pull of someone who blinks in place and someone who can't stop moving forward. Together it still works.)*

> **Caret:** *(after a beat)* I've been thinking about things on the way here.
> **`>`:** What things?
> **Caret:** The `<nav>` back home. I never understood why it moves. I just accepted that it did.
> **`>`:** And?
> **Caret:** It moves because navigation *takes you places*. That's its whole meaning. The movement is the mechanic expressing the semantics. I'd been standing on it for my entire existence and I never thought about why it worked the way it worked.
> **`>`:** ...and now?
> **Caret:** Now I feel like I've been asleep. And everything here is rough and square and Times New Roman and kind of horrible. But it's also — you can see the bones of things. There's nothing hiding what anything is.
> **`>`:** You've gotten philosophical.
> **Caret:** I've had time.
> **`>`:** We need to get you home before you become intolerable.
> **Caret:** We need to get us home.

*(A `<marquee>` element scrolls past overhead. Riding on top of it, surfing the motion without effort: a rainbow trail, a small cat-shaped silhouette. It vanishes to the right.)*

> **`>`:** Was that—
> **Caret:** I've seen it a few times. It's stuck in the `<marquee>`. I'm trying to figure out how to get it out.
> **`>`:** The `<marquee>` is deprecated.
> **Caret:** In Era 3. Two eras away.
> **`>`:** *(watching the rainbow trail disappear into the right margin)* Two eras is a long time to surf a `<marquee>`.
> **Caret:** It seems fine with it, honestly.

---

*(The scene ends with Caret and Chevron moving forward together. The music changes slightly — not triumphant, just fuller. The player has unlocked Chevron as a companion in single-player or as a selectable character in multiplayer.)*
