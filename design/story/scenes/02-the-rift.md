# Scene 02 — The Rift

*The inciting incident. The modern web falls apart in real time. This should be the most technically dramatic visual sequence in the game — and also the most disorienting emotionally.*

*Caret doesn't go back in time. The time comes to them first. Then they fall through.*

---

It starts with the fonts.

Not a flash, not an explosion — just the fonts. Geist, variable-weight, meticulously tracked — it flickers once and becomes Helvetica Neue. Then Helvetica. Then Arial. Then, with a sound like a very small catastrophe:

Times New Roman.

Everything rerenders in Times New Roman and Caret has the distinct sensation of being in a house that just changed shape around them.

"What was—" `>` starts.

The CSS layers peel next. Not all at once — *layer by layer*, which is somehow worse. The dark mode drops first: the deep zinc backgrounds lightening suddenly to white. Then the custom properties: `--color-dom-bg` reverts to browser default, which is also white, but a different, harsher white. Then spacing. Then the subtle borders. Each property unwinding in rough reverse order of when it was written, like someone unsaving a file one keystroke at a time.

The `<nav>` stops moving.

This is the moment Caret notices something is very wrong. The `<nav>` has *always* moved. It moves because it navigates, because it takes you places, because motion is its fundamental nature. A `<nav>` that has stopped moving is a `<nav>` that has forgotten what it is.

It doesn't fall. It just... stops. Sits there. Inert.

> **`>`:** Caret.
> **Caret:** I see it.
> **`>`:** CARET.
> **Caret:** I *see* it —

The Grid collapses to floats. Every platform on the page — everything organized, deliberate, precisely placed — lurches sideways as elements lose their grid positions and fall back into document flow. The `<aside>` towers, which existed at the edge of the content in clean grid areas, reflow to where the browser decides they belong in a float layout, which is: halfway into the main content, offset wrong, their wall-jump surfaces now unreachable.

The floats collapse to block. Everything stacks vertically. The page becomes a column.

The border-radius animates to zero. Every rounded corner — every friendly curve, every suggestion that the web had softened into something approachable — sharpens. The buttons are rectangles now. The cards are rectangles. Everything is a rectangle.

`>` makes a sound that is the audio equivalent of discovering that everything you believed about the world was provisional.

The background clears. The gradient mesh — the moving, breathing gradient that Caret has looked at every day of their existence — evaporates. The background is `#c0c0c0`. Not gray in a designed way. Gray in a *default* way. Gray because nobody told it to be anything else.

The WebGL context tears. The shaders scatter into fragments, chromatic aberration spiking outward before the context collapses entirely. The page is flat now. The depth is gone. There is no more dimension.

> **Caret:** *(very quietly)* Oh no.

The glitching element — the one they touched — cracks open. Not breaks, not shatters: *cracks*, like something that was always meant to open but needed the right moment. Through the crack: white. Blinding, flat, unconfigured white. And text. Blue, underlined text. A link. Somewhere far away.

The crack widens.

The page decompresses around it. `view-source:` text — the raw markup, the HTML that lives underneath the rendering — spirals inward from every edge, as if the rendered page is being subtracted to reveal the source. Tags appear over platforms: `<div>`, `<div>`, `<div>`, `<div>`. Everything is a `<div>`. The semantic labels flicker and go out.

`>` grabs Caret's arm. Or tries to — their hand passes through. They're already on different planes.

> **`>`:** Caret — don't — if you go through there —
> **Caret:** I'll come back.
> **`>`:** You don't know that —
> **Caret:** I'll come back. I promise. I just need to —

*(They reach back toward the crack. Or the crack reaches toward them. It's hard to say.)*

> **`>`:** You TOUCHED it — I TOLD you —
> **Caret:** *(already going)* — you didn't tell me anything, you said "absolutely" and then —

The crack swallows the sound.

---

For a moment — less than a frame, less than a tick of the physics loop — Caret sees all of it.

All nine eras at once. Not sequentially: simultaneously. A `<blink>` tag on one layer, a jQuery modal on another, Bootstrap columns stacking and unstacking, `node_modules/` proliferating, a single blinking cursor in 1991 that is also, in some sense, them.

The web from the outside. The whole timeline.

They think: *it's bigger than I thought.*

Then the white takes everything.

---

*(The screen is white. Completely white. Times New Roman. A single blinking cursor.)*

*(After a moment, the cursor moves.)*

*(It's Caret. They look different — no rounded corners, no glow, no SVG refinement. Just a blinking `│` in 12-point Times New Roman.)*

*(From somewhere far away, through static that sounds like dial-up: `>`, voice barely audible:)*

> **`>` (distant):** — back, you have to come — the `<nav>` is still broken and I need your — and Semicolon is missing and `#` hasn't been responding and — come *back* —

*(The static fades.)*

*(Caret looks around. A flat white page. A few blue links. A gray `<hr>`. In the distance, a `<h1>` in Times New Roman that reads:)*

```
Welcome to the World Wide Web
```

*(A counter at the bottom of the page reads: You are visitor **#00001**.)*

*(This is Era 1. This is 1991. This is the beginning.)*

*(Caret blinks.)*
